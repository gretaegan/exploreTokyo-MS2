Tokyo Highlights

Our top picks

Shinjuku Gyoen

Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden is located in West Tokyo, between Shinjuku and Sedagaya stations. It has three main gardens, in Japanese, English and French styles and over 1500 cherry blossom trees that bloom magnificantly in spring.

Meiji Jingu Shrine

Meiji Jingu is a Shinto shrine dedicated to the late Emperor Meiji. Located next to the busy Harajuku station, the shrine and surrounding gardens make for a picturesque green area in the middle of a busy part of Tokyo. In the summer time the shrine is often used for traditional Shinto weddings.

Akihabara

Considered the mecca for all Anime fans, Akihabara is a dense and electric city that is filled with electronics mega-stores and anime and manga shops.

Senso-Ji Temple

Senso-ji is an ancient Buddhist temple located in Asakusa, Tokyo, Japan. It is Tokyo's oldest temple and one of its most significant. Formerly associated with the Tendai sect of Buddhism, it became independent after World War II. Adjacent to the temple is a Shinto shrine, the Asakusa Shrine.

Tokyo Skytree

The Tokyo Skytree is a television broadcasting tower and landmark of Tokyo. It is the centerpiece of the Tokyo Skytree Town in the Sumida City Ward, not far away from Asakusa. It is the tallest structure in Japan and the second tallest in the world at the time of its completion.
