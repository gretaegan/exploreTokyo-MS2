Wether you are seeking a futuristic city adventure, or a historically rich tradiitonal escape, this is a city with something for everybody.

About Tokyo

With its futuristic skyscrapers, unrivaled food scene, and wild nightlife, Tokyo is a rush of pure adrenaline. This vast and multifaceted city is famously cutting edge, yet its ancient Buddhist temples, vintage teahouses, and peaceful gardens offer a serene escape — and a poignant reminder of the city’s long history. And for those who know where to look, Tokyo’s smaller pleasures (secret ramen spots, shopping alleys, chill record bars) are often hiding in plain sight.
Originally a fishing village named Edo, Tokyo is now the political and economic capital of Japan, and the Tokyo Metropolitan area the most populated city on the planet.

Helpful Hints

  • The currency in Japan is the Japanese Yen (pronounced 'en'). ATM machines are not abundantly found, and not all cater to Western credit cards so be sure to bring some cash!
  • Tipping is not commonplace in Japan, and is also considered rude. So its best just to pay the bill as it is.
  • On entry to a more traditional guesthouse or restaurant you might be asked to remove your shoes and put on a pair of slippers instead.
  • Talking loudly on the subway is considered rude, as is eating on the street!
  • While the official language spoken is Japanese, all subway and street signs are in English too, and many Japanese are happy to practise their English on you.
