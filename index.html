About Tokyo

With its futuristic skyscrapers, unrivaled food scene, and wild nightlife, Tokyo is a rush of pure adrenaline. This vast and multifaceted city is famously cutting edge, yet its ancient Buddhist temples, vintage teahouses, and peaceful gardens offer a serene escape — and a poignant reminder of the city’s long history. And for those who know where to look, Tokyo’s smaller pleasures are often hiding in plain sight.
Originally a fishing village named Edo, Tokyo is now the political and economic capital of Japan, and the Tokyo Metropolitan area the most populated city on the planet.
Made up of 23 special wards, each neighbourhood in Tokyo is different to the next one. From the richly traditional and historical areas like Asakusa, to the ultra-modern and high-rise areas like Shinjuku, this city is a juxtaposition of history meeting modern-day.
National parks and Shinto shrines sit amoungst busy shopping districts, and some of the subway stations are the busiest on earth.
